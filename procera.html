Goodyera procera

Fig: Goodyera procera

  • Family:         Orchidaceae
  • Sub family:  Orchidoideae
  • Tribe:            Neottieae
  • Sub tribe:     Spiranthinae
  • Genus:          Goodyera
  • Species:        procera

CHARACTERSTICS

Terrestrial herbs, 25-60cm high. Roots thick, tufted. Stems cylindrical, pale green, internodes 3.2-6.8 cm long, part covered with leaf sheaths. Leaves are green and rosette, ovate-elliptic, acute or sub-acuminate, 6-14 X 3-6cm, with stout midrib; petioles 3-4cm with dilated sheath. Flowers 3mm across, white in dense spike, spike 6-8cm long, Floral bract ovate-acuminate or lanceolate, sepales obtuse, pale green; dorsal sepal concave, lateral sepals oblong-lanceolate; lip cymbiform with infolded margins and deflexed notched apex, 1.3 X 1 mm, pale green, with 2 subglobular calli in the middle and two rows of seate in the sac within; column 1.5mm long, 10mm thick, white, erect, with slightly transverse depression below the stigma on ventral side and below the anther on dorsal side; anther 0.7 X 0.5mm, two chambered; pollinia 2, elongated, sessile; ovary resupinate, pale green subsessile.


IMPORTANCE

⦁ A suitable candidate for garden flower.

⦁ Flowering time : April-May.
