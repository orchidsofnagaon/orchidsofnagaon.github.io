Rhynchostylis retusa (L.) Blum.

Fig: Rhynchostylis retusa (L.) Blum.

(Image Source: efloraofindia)

  • Family:         Orchidaceae
  • Sub family:  Orchidoideae
  • Tribe:            Epidendreae
  • Sub tribe:     Sarcanthinae
  • Genus:        Rhynchostylis
  • Species:        retusa

CHARACTERSTICS

Epiphytic Orchid. Plants erect, leafy, producing densly flowered de-curved inflorescence. Stem unbranched, coated with leaf-sheaths, 15-30cm long. Leaves 12-16, distichus, oblong, 18-30 x 3-5cm. Inflorescence racemes with densely many flowered, 40-50cm long. Flowers 2-2.5cm across, rose to whitish pink; sepals and petals are white spotted purple; dorsal sepals obtuse, ovate to oblong, 8.5 x 5.5mm; lateral sepals subacute or obtuse,obliquely and broadly ovate, 8.5 x 8.5mm; petals oblong-ovate to oblong elliptic, 8 x 5mm; lip pale mauve; column 4-6mm long; anther 2-celled; pollinia 2, globose-pyriform.


IMPORTANCE

⦁ High aesthetic value, collector's delightful species.

⦁ Flowering time : March-May.
