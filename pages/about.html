ABOUT THE PROJECT

Around 32 species of ORCHIDS are found in the district of Nagaon (Assam, India). Most of the species of orchids are of genus Acampe, Aerides, Arundina, Bulbophylum, Cymbidium, Dendrobium, Eria, Geodorum, Goodyera, Liparis, Lusia, Malaxis, Papilionanthe, Phaius, Rhynchostylis, Spathoglottis and Zeuxine.

The main objectives of this project is collection of data of all the orchids species of Nagaon district through field survey, construction of an online database and to determine the evolutionary relationship between all the species by phylogenetic analysis and tree construction.

This project is carried out jointly by Hirakjyoti Acharjya and Pranjal Mahanta of B.Sc Biotechnology 6th Semester, under the mentorship of Mr. Parag Nath, Asst. Professor, Department of Biotechnology, Kaliabor College.
