Spathoglottis plicata Blume.

Fig: Spathoglottis plicata Blume.

(Image Source: efloraofindia)

  • Family:         Orchidaceae
  • Sub family:  Orchidoideae
  • Tribe:            Epidendreae
  • Sub tribe:     Bletiine
  • Genus:         Spathoglottis
  • Species:       plicata

CHARACTERSTICS

Terrestrial orchid, 62.5cm-75cm in height. Pseudobulbs ovoid in shape, completely covered by leaf sheaths and fleshy bracts. Leaves 5-8 acute, subacute, at apex, many nerved, oblong, lanceolate, 20-50 X 3-8cm, petioles with sheaths at base, 15-30cm long. Inflorescence raceme bearing 10-12 flowers, 50-80cm long. Flowers 5-8cm across, radish purple, floral bracts large, ovate; dorsal sepals elliptic, ovate, sessile, 2.3 x 1.0cm; lateral sepals clawed, ovate-lanceolate, 2.5 x 1cm. Petals pink, elliptic, acute at apex, 2.5 x 1.5cm; lip sessile, trilobed, 1.5-2cm long; column thin, winged, bent towards the lip; anther depressed, two chambered, 1.8 x 2.3mm; pollinia 8, on a common branch, gland present. Ovary not resupinate.


IMPORTANCE

⦁ A suitable candidate for gardening.

⦁ Flowering time : June-July.


GEOGRAPHICAL DISTRIBUTION

⦁ Amsoi

⦁ Chikoni Hills

⦁ Diju valley

⦁ Kaziranga National Park
