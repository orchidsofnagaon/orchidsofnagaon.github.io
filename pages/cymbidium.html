Cymbidium aloifolium (L.) Sw.

Fig: Cymbidium aloifolium (L.) Sw.

(Image Source: efloraofindia)

  • Family:         Orchidaceae
  • Sub family:  Orchidoideae
  • Tribe:            Epidendreae
  • Sub tribe:     Cymbidiane
  • Genus:          Cymbidium
  • Species:       aloifolium

CHARACTERSTICS

Epiphyte on tree trunk in huge clumps. Pseudobulb stout, linear-oblong, 30-40cm long. Inflorescence racemes, raceme pendulous, long, bearing several flowers. Flowers 3.5cm across,creamy yellow with purplish-red blotches; sepals and petals subequal; sepals oblong-lanceolate, 2-2.5 x 0.8-1.0 cm; petals elliptic-oblong, 2-2.5 x 0.8-1.0 cm; lip trilobed, erect, lanceolate 2.1 x 1.6cm; column erect, slender; pollinia 2, ovoid.


IMPORTANCE

⦁ A good cut flower, pseudobulbs are used as medicine in tribal society.

⦁ Flowering time : May-June.


GEOGRAPHICAL DISTRIBUTION

⦁ Amsoi

⦁ Chikoni Hills

⦁ Diju valley

⦁ Hatimura Hill

⦁ Kaziranga National Park

⦁ Laokhowa Wildlife Sanctuary

⦁ Pabitora Wildlife Sanctuary
